Business Agnostic Organisational Governance Frameworks Catalogue ()
Definition: Organisational Governance Frameworks Catalogue ABB is a grouping that covers a set of business objects that enable the protection, confidentiality, and interoperability of data, information, and knowledge assets and the organizational resources handling them. It includes the Security Framework, Privacy Framework, and Interoperability Framework. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Organisational Governance Frameworks Catalogue ABB is a comprehensive collection of frameworks that are designed to guide organizations in managing and protecting their data and information assets. The Security Framework focuses on the protection of these assets and the resources handling them. The Privacy Framework, on the other hand, emphasizes the confidentiality aspects of these assets and resources. Lastly, the Interoperability Framework is designed to enable organizations to collaborate effectively towards the joint delivery of public services. It does this by specifying a set of common elements such as vocabulary, concepts, principles, guidelines, and recommendations. Example: An example of the application of the Organisational Governance Frameworks Catalogue ABB could be a public service organization that needs to ensure the security and privacy of its data while also needing to collaborate with other organizations. The Security Framework would guide the organization in protecting its data and resources. The Privacy Framework would help ensure the confidentiality of the data. The Interoperability Framework would provide the necessary guidelines and recommendations for effective collaboration with other organizations.
