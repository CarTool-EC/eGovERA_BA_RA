Governance Analyst Data Spaces Concerns ()
Definition: Governance Analyst Data Spaces Concerns ABB is a Grouping that covers the processes a governance analyst should follow, and the relation/influence of process related with other roles' processes, to read/assess/understand an eGovERA model in order to understand/develop a Data Spaces solution/architecture. It includes elements such as Organisational view and Legal view. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The 'Governance Analyst Data Spaces Concerns ABB' is a comprehensive catalogue of elements that a governance analyst needs to consider when developing a Data Spaces solution or architecture. It includes the 'Organisational view', which pertains to understanding the structure, roles, and responsibilities within the organization, and the 'Legal view', which involves understanding the legal and regulatory requirements that the solution or architecture must comply with. These elements are crucial in ensuring that the solution or architecture is not only technically sound but also aligns with the organization's goals and complies with all relevant laws and regulations. Example: For instance, when developing a Data Spaces solution for a government agency, the governance analyst would first look at the 'Organisational view' to understand the agency's structure, roles, and responsibilities. This could involve analyzing the agency's organizational chart, job descriptions, and other relevant documents. Next, the analyst would consider the 'Legal view' to ensure that the solution complies with all relevant laws and regulations. This could involve reviewing the agency's legal and regulatory compliance policies, consulting with legal experts, and conducting a legal risk assessment.
