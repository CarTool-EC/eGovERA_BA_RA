Directive 2014/24/EU of the European Parliament and of the Council of 26 February 2014 ()
Description: Directive 2014/24/EU is a European Union legislation that governs public procurement processes within member states. It was adopted on 26 February 2014 and replaces Directive 2004/18/EC. The directive aims to promote transparency, competition, and value for money in public procurement by setting out rules and procedures that must be followed by public authorities when awarding contracts for goods, services, and works. It also introduces measures to simplify and streamline the procurement process, encourage the participation of small and medium-sized enterprises, and promote sustainable and socially responsible procurement practices.
