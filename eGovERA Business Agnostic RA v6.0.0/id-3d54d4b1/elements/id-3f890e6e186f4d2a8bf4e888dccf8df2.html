Business Agnostic Data Space Digital Public Services Catalogue ()
Definition: Business Agnostic Data Space Digital Public Services Catalogue ABB is a grouping that covers a range of digital public services that are not specific to any business sector. These services include eSignature, eNotification, Log Management, Audit, eIdentification, Digital Collaboration and a sub-catalogue of services for Business Agnostic Data Space Participants. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The catalogue is a comprehensive collection of digital public services that are designed to be applicable across various business sectors. These services include eSignature, which provides digital signature services; eNotification, which provides digital notification services; Log Management, which provides digital log management services; Audit, which provides digital audit services; eIdentification, which provides digital identification services; and Digital Collaboration, which provides digital collaboration services. The catalogue also includes a sub-catalogue of services specifically for Business Agnostic Data Space Participants, which includes services such as Data Extraction, Transformation and Loading; Content Management; Open Data Management; Workflow Management; Data Exchange; eArchiving; Anonymisation; and Secure Access Control. Example: For instance, a business in the healthcare sector could use the eSignature service to digitally sign documents, the eNotification service to send digital notifications to patients, the Log Management service to manage digital logs of patient records, the Audit service to conduct digital audits of their operations, the eIdentification service to digitally identify patients, and the Digital Collaboration service to collaborate digitally with other healthcare providers. They could also use the services in the Business Agnostic Data Space Participant sub-catalogue to manage their data and workflows.
