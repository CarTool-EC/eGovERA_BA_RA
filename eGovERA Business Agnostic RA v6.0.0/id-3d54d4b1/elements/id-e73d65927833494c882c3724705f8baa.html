Directive (EU) 2015/2366 of the European Parliament and of the Council of 25 November 2015 ()
Description: Directive (EU) 2015/2366, also known as the Payment Services Directive (PSD), is a European Union directive that was adopted in 2015 to regulate payment services within the internal market of the EU. It aims to create a single market for payment services by harmonizing rules and regulations across member states. The directive provides a legal framework for payment service providers, including banks and other financial institutions, and sets out rules for payment transactions, consumer rights, and the security of electronic payments. The PSD is an evolution of the initial Directive 2007/64/EC to adapt to technological advancements and changes in the payment industry.
