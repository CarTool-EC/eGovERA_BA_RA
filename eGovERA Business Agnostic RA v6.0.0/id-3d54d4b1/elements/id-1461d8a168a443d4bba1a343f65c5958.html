Constraints, Assumptions and Decisions ()
Definition: Constraints, Assumptions and Decisions ABB is a Grouping that covers a broad range of information entities that are crucial in the planning, execution, and evaluation of any project or process. These elements help in understanding the limitations, preconditions, and choices made during the lifecycle of a project or process. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Constraints, Assumptions and Decisions ABB catalogue is a comprehensive collection of elements that are integral to any project or process. 'Constraints' refer to the limitations or restrictions that may affect the execution or outcome of a project. 'Assumptions' are the conditions or facts that are assumed to be true in the planning and execution of a project. 'Decisions' are the choices made during the project lifecycle, which can significantly influence the project's direction and outcome. These elements are crucial as they help in identifying potential risks, planning for contingencies, and making informed decisions. They also provide a framework for evaluating the project's success and learning for future projects.
