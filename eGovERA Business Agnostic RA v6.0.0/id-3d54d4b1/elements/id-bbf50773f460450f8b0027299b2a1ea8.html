Interoperable Europe Act Proposal ()
Description: The Interoperable Europe Act is a proposal for a regulation that aims to establish a high level of public sector interoperability across the European Union. The regulation seeks to promote the seamless exchange of data and information between public sector entities, including national, regional, and local authorities, as well as public service providers. The proposal outlines a framework for achieving interoperability, including the development of common standards, the use of open data, and the establishment of a European Interoperability Framework. The regulation also includes provisions for ensuring data protection and privacy, as well as measures to promote transparency and accountability in the use of public sector data. Overall, the Interoperable Europe Act aims to improve the efficiency and effectiveness of public services across the EU by facilitating the exchange of information and data between different public sector entities.
