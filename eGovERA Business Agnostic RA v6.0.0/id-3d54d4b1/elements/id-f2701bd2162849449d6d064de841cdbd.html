Directive 2014/55/EU of the European Parliament and of the Council of 16 April 2014 ()
Description: Directive 2014/55/EU, also known as the Directive on electronic invoicing in public procurement, is a legislation enacted by the European Parliament and Council on 16 April 2014. The directive aims to establish a common framework for the use of electronic invoicing in public procurement across the European Union. It sets out the requirements for the format, content, and transmission of electronic invoices, with the goal of promoting efficiency, cost savings, and interoperability in public procurement processes. The directive also encourages the use of electronic invoicing by public authorities and promotes the adoption of standardized electronic invoicing solutions.
