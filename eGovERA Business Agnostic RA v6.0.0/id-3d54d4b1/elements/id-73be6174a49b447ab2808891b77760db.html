Business Agnostic Data Space Participant Digital Public Service Catalog ()
Definition: Business Agnostic Data Space Participant Digital Public Service Catalog (ABB) is a grouping that covers a range of digital public services that are essential for the operation and management of data in a business-agnostic manner. These services include data extraction, transformation and loading, content management, open data management, workflow management, data exchange, eArchiving, anonymisation, and secure access control. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Business Agnostic Data Space Participant Digital Public Service Catalog ABB is a comprehensive catalog that encompasses various digital public services that are not specific to any business or industry. These services are crucial for the efficient handling and management of data. 'Data Extraction, Transformation and Loading' refers to the process of extracting data from different sources, transforming it into a usable format, and loading it into a database. 'Content Management' involves the creation, management, and modification of digital content. 'Open Data Management' is about managing data that is freely available to everyone to use and republish. 'Workflow Management' is the coordination of tasks that make up a work process. 'Data Exchange' is the process of transferring data from one system to another. 'eArchiving' is the storage of digital data for long-term preservation. 'Anonymisation' is the process of removing personally identifiable information from data sets. 'Secure Access Control' is about managing access to resources in a computing environment. Example: Examples of application include a government agency using the 'Open Data Management' service to manage and share public data, a healthcare organization using 'Anonymisation' service to protect patient data, or a corporation using 'Workflow Management' to streamline its business processes.
