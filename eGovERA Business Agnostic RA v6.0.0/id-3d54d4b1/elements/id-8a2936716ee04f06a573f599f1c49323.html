Business Agnostic Data Policy Catalogue ()
Definition: Business Agnostic Data Policy Catalogue ABB is a grouping that covers a range of policies and guidelines designed to manage and control data assets within an organization. These policies ensure seamless interoperability between different systems, maintain data quality, govern data usage, and manage data within a blockchain network. The catalogue is business agnostic, meaning it can be applied across various industries and business models. Source: EIRA Team Research Additional information: The Business Agnostic Data Policy Catalogue ABB includes four main elements: Interoperability Policy, Data Quality Framework, Data Governance Guidelines, and Blockchain Policy. The Interoperability Policy ensures that different systems and data within an organization or across entities can work together seamlessly. The Data Quality Framework provides a structured approach to assess, measure, and improve data quality. The Data Governance Guidelines provide direction for managing and controlling data assets, ensuring data quality, integrity, privacy, security, and compliance. Lastly, the Blockchain Policy governs the management, access, and utilization of data within a blockchain network, ensuring integrity, immutability, and transparency. Example: For instance, a healthcare organization can use the Interoperability Policy to ensure that its various systems, such as electronic health records and billing systems, can communicate effectively. The Data Quality Framework can be used to ensure that the data collected from patients is accurate and reliable. The Data Governance Guidelines can help the organization manage its data assets, ensuring privacy and compliance with regulations like HIPAA. The Blockchain Policy can be used if the organization decides to use blockchain technology for secure and transparent data storage and transactions.
